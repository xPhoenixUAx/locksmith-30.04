Service scope

Residential lock and access requests, explained

Residential requests often involve access to a home, apartment, garage entry, exterior door, interior door, mailbox lock, or smart lock system. Users may be looking for a provider conversation about rekeying after a move, replacing worn hardware, discussing smart lock compatibility, or addressing a broken key or lock that no longer operates smoothly.

Because home access work affects security and personal property, users should ask providers to explain the proposed scope in plain language before any work begins. LockBridge Connect helps route the request, but the independent provider is responsible for discussing available service options, terms, and requirements.

Standards users should ask about

  • Provider license or registration requirements in the local jurisdiction.
  • Proof of residence or authorization before home access work.
  • Written pricing for service call, labor, parts, smart lock setup, or rekeying.
  • Warranty terms for hardware or labor when a provider offers them.

Information to prepare

  • Property type, ZIP code, and preferred appointment timing.
  • Whether the request involves a lockout, rekey, replacement, or smart lock.
  • Door type, approximate lock type, and whether parts may be needed.
  • Any landlord, HOA, tenant, or property manager approval requirements.
