Service scope

Commercial lock, door hardware, and access requests

Commercial requests can involve offices, retail spaces, warehouses, rental properties, shared workspaces, or managed buildings. Common conversations include business lockouts, rekeying after employee turnover, replacing worn commercial hardware, reviewing access control concerns, or planning a scheduled provider consultation.

Commercial access work can affect staff, tenants, customers, inventory, and building operations. LockBridge Connect helps route the request to independent local providers who may be available to discuss scope, authorization, documentation, scheduling, and pricing.

Standards users should ask about

  • Business identity, provider credentials, and licensing where applicable.
  • Written authorization from an owner, manager, tenant, or approved representative.
  • Itemized pricing for service call, labor, hardware, scheduling, and follow-up work.
  • Warranty, documentation, key control, and access handoff procedures.

Information to prepare

  • Business address, access point, and type of commercial property.
  • Whether the request is a lockout, rekey, replacement, hardware issue, or consultation.
  • Names of authorized decision makers and on-site contact details.
  • Preferred service window and any building access rules.
