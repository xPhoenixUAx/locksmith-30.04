By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies in accordance with this policy, except where consent is required by applicable law.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to ensure websites function properly, improve performance, and provide information to website operators.

Cookies can be:

  • Session cookies: temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser
  • Persistent cookies: remain on your device until deleted or expired
  • First-party cookies: set by our website
  • Third-party cookies: set by external services or providers

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the operation of the website and cannot be disabled. They include cookie consent cookies that remember your preferences and session management cookies that maintain your session and request flow.

3.2 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how users interact with the platform so we can improve usability and performance. Examples may include Google Analytics, which tracks traffic, page views, and general usage patterns, and performance monitoring tools that help identify technical issues.

All analytics data is collected in an aggregated and anonymized form where possible.

3.3 Functional Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and improve user experience. Examples may include language preferences and temporary form data storage that helps prevent data loss.

3.4 Marketing Cookies

These cookies may be used to measure marketing effectiveness and deliver relevant advertisements. Examples may include advertising platforms such as Google Ads and remarketing technologies that help display relevant ads across websites. These cookies are used only where permitted and, where required, based on user consent.

4. Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that set cookies on your device, including analytics providers such as Google Analytics, advertising networks such as Google Ads, and social media platforms.

These third parties may collect information about your online activities across different websites. We do not control these cookies. Please review the privacy and cookie policies of these providers for more information.

5. How to Manage Cookies

You have the right to accept or reject non-essential cookies.

5.1 Cookie Banner / Consent

Where required by law, such as under the GDPR or ePrivacy Directive, we will request your consent before placing non-essential cookies.

5.2 Browser Settings

You can manage cookies through your browser settings, including Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Safari, and Microsoft Edge. Blocking cookies may affect website functionality.

5.3 Opt-Out Tools

You may opt out of certain tracking tools, including the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on, Digital Advertising Alliance tools, and Network Advertising Initiative tools.

6. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature. Currently, there is no widely accepted standard for interpreting DNT signals, and our website does not respond to them.
